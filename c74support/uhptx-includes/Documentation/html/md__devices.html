UHEV1

The image below illustrates the main components of the device:

The transducer board produces and focuses the ultrasound onto your hand to create the haptic feedback. The Leap Motion Controller cradle holds a Leap Motion Controller in a pre-calibrated position. This allows the Ultrahaptics Demo application to work straight out of the box. If you wish, you can remove the Leap Motion Controller cradle and integrate your own choice of tracking solution. Just turn the device over and remove these screws with a hex key.

The Status LED displays the current state of the UHEV1.

The UHEV1 is connected to a computer via the micro USB connector. All of the data transmission and ultrasound focusing is taken care of by the SDK. This leaves you free to focus on your own experimentation and design using the API.

The power connector is where you connect the provided power supply. UHEV1 uses a 20V power supply.

WARNING
There are two power connectors on the side of the device. One of these should be covered with a sticker. Only connect the power supply to the uncovered socket adjacent to the micro USB port.

Power Polarity

It shouldn't happen, but it is possible for the tip of the power supply to fall out. If this happens it must be reinserted with the correct orientation. All Ultrahaptics arrays are centre-positive so you should align letters ‘CEN’ on the tip with the positive symbol on the receptacle. as shown in this photo.

UHDK5

The components of the UHDK5 are similar to those of the UHEV1.

UHDK5 uses a 12V power supply and only has one power port. Like UHEV1 it uses a micro USB connector for data. These ports can be found on the underside of the device.

USX

The components of the USX are similar to those of the UHDK5.

USX uses a 24V power supply and only has one power port. Like UHDK5 and UHEV1 it uses a micro USB connector for data. These ports can be found on the underside of the device.

The Leap Motion Controller cradle is the same as that attached to the UHDK5.

WARNING
Only remove the transducer board if instructed by an Ultrahaptics representative and only in a static free environment to avoid damaging the circuit underneath.
